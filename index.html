About Me

Hi, I’m Mike Murillo, an early years and primary teacher from Chicago, currently based in Chengdu, China. I have spent more than 10 years teaching young learners in China and Southeast Asia, especially multilingual students who are building confidence in English, early literacy, and classroom communication.

My teaching style is warm, structured, and active. I believe young children need to feel safe and understood before they can take real learning risks, but they also need clear routines, steady expectations, and plenty of chances to practice. In my classroom, I use movement, songs, stories, visuals, games, and hands-on activities to help students stay engaged and build independence.

A major part of my recent teaching has focused on early literacy and phonics. I enjoy helping children connect sounds to letters, blend CVC words, use decodable reading routines, and grow from “I can’t read this” to “Let me try.” This portfolio shares selected evidence from my teaching practice, Moreland University clinical work, lesson planning, assessment, family communication, and reflection.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe young children learn best in classrooms where they feel safe, known, and gently challenged. My teaching is built around clear routines, warm relationships, movement, language practice, and repeated chances to succeed. For multilingual learners, especially in the early years, confidence grows when students have enough structure to understand what is expected and enough encouragement to keep trying.

A major part of my work is helping children build strong foundations in language and literacy. I use songs, stories, visuals, phonics routines, CVC blending, decodable reading practice, games, and one-to-one support to make early reading feel possible. I also believe good teaching requires careful noticing. I use observation, student work, informal checks, and family communication to understand what students need next and adjust my instruction with patience, fairness, and care.
